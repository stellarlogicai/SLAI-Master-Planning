# SLAI Crisis Management

Version: 1.0

---

# Philosophy

Bad days are inevitable.

What defines SLAI is not whether mistakes occur.

It is:

How quickly we acknowledge them.

How transparently we communicate.

How responsibly we fix them.

---

# Crisis Priorities

Always in this order:

1. Protect People
2. Protect Customer Data
3. Restore Service
4. Communicate Clearly
5. Learn and Improve

---

# Data Breach

Immediate Actions:

* Freeze affected systems.
* Determine breach scope.
* Notify leadership.
* Begin forensic investigation.
* Notify affected customers.
* Publish facts only.

---

Never:

* Hide incidents.
* Delay notification unnecessarily.
* Blame customers.

---

# Major Service Failure

Priorities:

* Restore service.
* Communicate estimated recovery.
* Provide workarounds.
* Publish postmortem.

---

# AI Failure

If AI:

* Produces dangerous recommendations.
* Violates company values.
* Shows unstable behavior.

Actions:

* Disable affected model.
* Escalate to leadership.
* Investigate root cause.
* Retrain or replace.

---

Human safety always overrides automation.

---

# Founder Unavailable

If founder unavailable:

Leadership Team may:

* Continue operations.
* Approve normal expenses.
* Handle customer issues.

Leadership may NOT:

* Sell company.
* Change values.
* Accept investors.
* Alter compensation philosophy.

---

# Post Mortems

Every major incident receives:

* Timeline
* Root Cause
* Customer Impact
* Resolution
* Prevention Steps

Purpose:

Learn.

Not blame.
