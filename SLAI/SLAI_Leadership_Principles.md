# SLAI Leadership Principles

**Version:** 2.0

---

# Purpose

Leadership at SLAI is not about authority.

Leadership is about:

* Serving teams.
* Protecting company values.
* Helping employees grow.
* Removing obstacles.
* Building an environment where people can do their best work.

A leader succeeds when their team succeeds.

---

# Leaders Serve Teams

Leaders exist to:

* Remove roadblocks.
* Provide direction.
* Support career growth.
* Protect employees.
* Encourage learning.

Leaders do NOT exist to:

* Micromanage.
* Rule by fear.
* Take credit for team accomplishments.
* Create unnecessary stress.

Leadership is service.

---

# Remember What It Felt Like

Every leader should remember:

What it felt like to:

* Struggle financially.
* Feel undervalued.
* Be overlooked.
* Work hard without recognition.
* Feel replaceable.

Never become the boss you hated working for.

---

# Document Decisions

Important decisions should always be documented.

If it isn't documented:

* It can't be improved.
* It can't be referenced.
* It becomes tribal knowledge.

Documentation creates:

* Transparency.
* Consistency.
* Independence.
* Stability.

---

# Praise Publicly

Celebrate success.

Public praise:

* Builds confidence.
* Improves morale.
* Encourages growth.
* Reinforces good habits.

People remember being appreciated.

---

# Correct Privately

Mistakes should:

* Be discussed respectfully.
* Focus on improvement.
* Never become humiliation.

Public criticism destroys trust.

Private coaching builds it.

---

# Hire Slowly

Bad hires are expensive.

Hire for:

* Integrity
* Curiosity
* Ownership
* Communication
* Problem Solving
* Skill

Character is slightly more important than skill.

Skills can be taught.

Character usually cannot.

---

# Fire Respectfully

If separation becomes necessary:

* Be honest.
* Be respectful.
* Be direct.
* Preserve dignity.

Everyone deserves respect.

Even during difficult conversations.

---

# Protect Work-Life Balance

Employees are people first.

Encourage:

* Family time
* Vacations
* Personal hobbies
* Mental health
* Physical health

Burnout helps nobody.

Long-term consistency beats short-term intensity.

---

# Founder Sacrifices First

During difficult years:

Founder:

* Salary freezes first.
* Bonus disappears first.
* Expansion pauses first.

Employees:

Protected whenever reasonably possible.

---

Principle:

Founder sacrifices first.

Employees sacrifice last.

---

# Their Wins Are My Wins

Employees are not expenses.

They are partners in the mission.

When employees succeed:

The company succeeds.

When the company succeeds:

Employees should share in that success.

---

# Never Stop Learning

Curiosity is required.

SLAI encourages:

* Research
* Education
* Experimentation
* Reading
* Personal growth

The company should never stop learning.

Neither should its leaders.

---

# Founder Promise

I will never forget:

What it felt like to struggle.

I will never build:

The company I hated working for.

Success means:

Lifting others with me.

Their wins

are my wins.
