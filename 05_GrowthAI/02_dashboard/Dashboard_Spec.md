# GrowthAI Dashboard Specification

## Dashboard Purpose

The dashboard should tell Jamie exactly where customer acquisition stands without needing to dig through every lead.

## Dashboard Sections

### 1. Today's Growth Snapshot

Cards:

- New Leads
- Research Complete
- Outreach Drafted
- Contacted
- Responses
- Demos Booked
- Customers Won
- Revenue Added

### 2. Pipeline Overview

Display count of leads by status:

- New
- Research Needed
- Research Complete
- Outreach Drafted
- Contacted
- Responded
- Interested
- Demo Scheduled
- Trial
- Customer
- Lost
- Do Not Contact

### 3. Top Opportunities

Table columns:

- Business
- Industry
- Location
- Score
- Confidence
- Main Pain Point
- Recommended Action
- Status

### 4. Follow-Ups Needed

Table columns:

- Lead
- Last Contact Date
- Follow-Up Due Date
- Last Message
- Suggested Follow-Up
- Priority

### 5. AI Recommendations

Examples:

- "5 leads with outdated websites and no online booking should be prioritized."
- "Residential cleaning companies with 5-15 employees are converting best."
- "Price objections are common. Consider creating ROI explanation content."

### 6. Referral Program

Cards:

- Referrals This Month
- Referral Credits Issued
- Top Referrer
- Pending Referral Rewards

### 7. Weekly Review

Show:

- Best channel
- Worst channel
- Best converting lead type
- Biggest objection
- Most effective outreach template
- Next week recommended focus

## Dashboard Design Notes

Keep dashboard simple and founder-friendly.

Avoid vanity metrics unless they help make a decision.

Every dashboard section should answer:

> What should Jamie do next?
