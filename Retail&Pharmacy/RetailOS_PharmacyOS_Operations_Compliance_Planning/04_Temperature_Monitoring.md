# Temperature Monitoring

## Pain Point

Employees and managers must remember to check temperatures for walk-in coolers, freezers, and standing coolers.

Manual checks depend on memory, paper logs, and employees who are already interrupted by customers and store tasks.

## Feature Concept

Use Wi-Fi temperature probes to automatically monitor cold storage.

## Areas to Monitor

- Walk-in cooler
- Walk-in freezer
- Standing coolers
- Milk cooler
- Ice cream freezer
- Pharmacy refrigerator
- Vaccine refrigerator
- Backroom cooler/freezer

## Desired Workflow

1. Probe records temperature automatically.
2. Reading is sent to the system.
3. System logs timestamped history.
4. System compares reading to safe range.
5. Alert triggers if out of range.
6. Employee gets corrective steps.
7. Employee logs action taken.
8. Manager verifies if required.

## Example Alert

```text
Cooler temp high: 47°F
Location: Standing Cooler 2

Step 1: Check if door is fully closed.
Step 2: Confirm unit is running.
Step 3: Move affected product if required by policy.
Step 4: Notify manager.
Step 5: Log corrective action.
```
