# Product Principles for RetailOS / PharmacyOS

## 1. Do Not Replace Critical Backbones Too Early

Databases, compliance systems, reporting systems, and corporate infrastructure exist for a reason. The first opportunity is workflow improvement, not reckless replacement.

## 2. Workflow Hurts the Bottom Line

Bad workflow burns payroll through extra clicks, repeated lookups, unnecessary screen bouncing, missed tasks, training friction, interruptions, and avoidable mistakes.

## 3. Design Around Real Employees

Employees are interrupted. They are tired. They are handling customers. They should not need to memorize every rule.

## 4. One Task, One Clear Next Action

The system should guide one task at a time.

```text
What are you doing?
What do you scan?
What happens next?
Where does it go?
How do you finish?
```

## 5. Corporate Gets Data, Employees Get Workflow

The same task should satisfy multiple needs:

- Employee gets a simple process.
- Manager gets visibility.
- Corporate gets reporting.
- Compliance gets audit logs.

## 6. Hardware Should Be Used Better

If stores already have Android-based Zebra devices, scanners, cameras, Wi-Fi, local servers, and network infrastructure, the software should use those tools in a cleaner workflow.

## 7. AI Assists, Policy Governs

AI can summarize, detect patterns, recommend schedules, explain tasks, and flag risks.

AI should not override compliance policy, pharmacy rules, or manager/pharmacist authority.
