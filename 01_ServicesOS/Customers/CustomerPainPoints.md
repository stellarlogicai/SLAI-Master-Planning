# ServicesOS Customer Pain Points

## Purpose

This document lists the real problems ServicesOS should solve for cleaning businesses. Product decisions should connect back to these pain points.

## Core Pain Point

Cleaning businesses often operate with too many disconnected tools, manual processes, text messages, paper notes, and memory-based workflows.

This creates:

- Missed details
- Lost time
- Confused employees
- Payment delays
- Poor customer communication
- Inconsistent service quality
- Owner burnout

## Pain Point Categories

## 1. Customer Management

Problems:

- Customer information spread across texts, notes, spreadsheets, and phones
- Hard to find addresses, pet notes, access notes, and preferences
- Repeat customers require memory instead of structured records
- Family/friend discounts are not clearly documented

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Central customer profiles
- Property details
- Customer notes
- Pet/access notes
- Discount visibility
- Service history

## 2. Estimates and Quotes

Problems:

- Owners guess pricing manually
- Hard to explain pricing to customers
- Deep cleans and add-ons may be undercharged
- Family/friend pricing can create conflict if discounts are not visible
- Quotes may not reflect time, difficulty, pets, or special conditions

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Structured estimate builder
- Room/service-based pricing
- Add-ons
- Discount lines
- Notes explaining price
- Professional estimate delivery

## 3. Scheduling

Problems:

- Jobs scheduled manually
- Recurring jobs hard to track
- Rescheduling creates confusion
- Employee assignment may happen through texts
- No easy way to see workload by day/week

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Calendar/schedule board
- Recurring job support
- Employee assignments
- Job status tracking
- Schedule change visibility

## 4. Employee Coordination

Problems:

- Employees may not know where to go or what to do
- Job notes can get missed
- Office/owner gets repeated calls/texts
- New employees need repeated explanations
- Job completion proof may be missing

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Employee mobile app
- Today’s jobs
- Job details
- Checklists
- Photos
- Training
- Messaging
- Time tracking

## 5. Training

Problems:

- Training is inconsistent
- New hires learn differently depending on who trains them
- Processes may be explained repeatedly
- No proof that employee completed training
- Company-specific expectations may not be documented

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Training library
- Assigned modules
- Quizzes
- Completion tracking
- SOP access
- Future certification-based job assignment

## 6. Payments

Problems:

- Deposits and final payments tracked manually
- Customers delay payment
- Employees may not be able to collect payment in the field
- Owners may not have clean payment visibility
- Fees may be confusing

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Stripe payments
- Stripe Connect
- Deposits
- Final payments
- Tap to Pay
- Receipts
- Transparent fee breakdown

## 7. Communication

Problems:

- Important messages get lost in texts
- Customer instructions may not reach employees
- Employee issues may not reach the office quickly
- No central history of what happened

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Job-specific notes
- Employee-to-office messaging
- Customer communication templates
- Incident reporting
- Future AI summaries

## 8. Quality Control

Problems:

- Owner may not know if checklist was completed
- No photo proof
- Customer complaints become hard to verify
- Inconsistent service quality between employees

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Required checklists
- Before/after photos
- Completion timestamps
- Issue reporting
- Inspection templates

## 9. Too Many Subscriptions

Problems:

Cleaning companies may pay for separate tools for:

- CRM
- Scheduling
- Payments
- Contracts
- Training
- Messaging
- Payroll/time tracking
- File storage
- Forms

This creates extra cost and system switching.

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Combining core workflows into one platform
- Reducing duplicate entry
- Reducing subscription sprawl
- Keeping pricing fair and predictable

## 10. Owner Burnout

Problems:

Owners carry too much in their head:

- Customer preferences
- Employee schedules
- Pricing logic
- Job notes
- Follow-ups
- Payments
- Training

ServicesOS should solve by:

- Centralizing information
- Reducing admin work
- Making work visible
- Automating reminders and tracking
- Helping the owner delegate confidently

## Highest Priority Problems For MVP

The MVP should focus on:

1. Customers
2. Estimates
3. Scheduling
4. Employee assignments
5. Employee app job completion
6. Checklists
7. Payments
8. Training basics
9. Job notes and photos

## Problems To Solve Later

Later versions can solve:

- Advanced AI routing
- Payroll automation
- Inventory scanning
- Deep analytics
- Employee performance intelligence
- Customer portal
- AI support assistant
- Multi-service vertical expansion

## Customer Interview Questions

Ask:

1. What part of running your cleaning business wastes the most time?
2. What tools do you currently use?
3. What do you track manually?
4. What causes customer complaints?
5. What causes employee confusion?
6. What do you forget most often?
7. What do you wish software handled for you?
8. What would make you switch from your current tools?

## Success Signal

ServicesOS is solving real pain when customers say:

> I used to keep this in my head or across multiple apps. Now it is all in one place.
