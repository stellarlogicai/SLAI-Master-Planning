# SLAI Website Private Feedback Plan

## Purpose

The SLAI website should stay in private feedback mode until ServicesOS is stable enough for confident public positioning. The goal is not to “launch” publicly yet. The goal is to find confusion, trust gaps, and unclear messaging before wider exposure.

## Review Group

Private feedback group:

* Wife
* Trusted friends/family
* 1–2 trusted business-minded people if available

Do not use broad public posting yet.

## Feedback Goals

Ask all of the following:

1. Does the website make sense?
2. Does ServicesOS sound useful?
3. Would a cleaning business trust it?
4. What would stop someone from requesting early access?

## What Reviewers Should Focus On

### First Impression

Ask:

```text
Within 10 seconds, what do you think Stellar Logic AI does?
Within 10 seconds, what do you think ServicesOS does?
```

This checks whether the home page and ServicesOS page communicate clearly.

### Trust

Ask:

```text
Does this feel real and trustworthy?
What makes it feel trustworthy?
What makes it feel too early or unclear?
```

This checks whether the website feels credible without overclaiming.

### ServicesOS Usefulness

Ask:

```text
Would a cleaning business understand how this helps them?
What part sounds most useful?
What part sounds unnecessary?
What part sounds confusing?
```

This checks whether the ServicesOS messaging speaks to real small business pain.

### Conversion

Ask:

```text
Would you click Request Demo or Early Access?
If not, what would stop you?
What information would you need first?
```

This checks whether the site creates enough confidence to continue.

### Wife-Specific Questions

Ask:

```text
Would this help with a real cleaning business?
What would save you the most time?
What would annoy you?
What would you expect to be simpler?
What would need to work before you trusted it?
```

This is the most important feedback because she is closest to the first ServicesOS beta user.

## What Not To Ask

Avoid:

```text
Do you like it?
Does it look cool?
Is the design good?
```

Those questions create vague feedback.

Better:

```text
What confused you?
What did you expect to happen?
What would make you trust it more?
What would stop you from using it?
```

## Feedback Form

Use this simple structure:

```text
Reviewer:
Date:
Device used:
Page reviewed:

1. What does SLAI do?
2. What does ServicesOS do?
3. What felt clear?
4. What felt confusing?
5. What felt trustworthy?
6. What felt too early or vague?
7. Would you request demo/early access?
8. What would stop you?
9. What should be changed first?
10. Any wording that felt wrong?
```

## Founder Review Rules

When reviewing feedback:

* Do not defend the website.
* Do not explain what they “should have understood.”
* If multiple people misunderstand the same thing, the website is unclear.
* If one person dislikes a preference, note it but do not overreact.
* If your wife flags a ServicesOS workflow issue, treat it as high priority.
* If feedback asks for future products, capture it but do not distract from ServicesOS.

## What To Fix First

Priority order:

```text
1. Confusion about what ServicesOS does
2. Trust gaps
3. Unclear call-to-action
4. Overly broad SLAI messaging
5. Visual polish
6. Future product wording
```

## Public Launch Language

Do not say:

```text
We launched.
ServicesOS is live for everyone.
```

Use:

```text
Early ServicesOS testing is underway.
SLAI website is open for private feedback.
ServicesOS beta preparation is in progress.
```

## Success Criteria

The website is private-feedback ready if reviewers can answer:

```text
SLAI builds AI-powered operating systems for businesses.
ServicesOS helps service businesses manage quotes, customers, scheduling, and operations.
The product is early but credible.
The next step is requesting early access or giving feedback.
```

## Next Website Step

After private feedback:

1. Collect common confusion points.
2. Update wording.
3. Improve ServicesOS page first.
4. Do not expand GrowthAI/EducationOS/FutureAI pages heavily until ServicesOS beta is stable.
