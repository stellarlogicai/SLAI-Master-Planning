# ServicesOS Pricing Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines the pricing philosophy for ServicesOS so pricing stays fair, predictable, profitable, and aligned with SLAI values.

## Pricing Philosophy

ServicesOS should be priced to create long-term trust, not short-term extraction.

The goal is:

- Fair pricing
- Transparent fees
- No surprise charges
- Healthy profit margins
- Pricing that scales with business size and usage
- Enough revenue to support development, employees, infrastructure, and growth

ServicesOS should not simply add up the cost of every subscription it replaces and charge that amount. The value is high, but the pricing should still feel fair to small businesses.

## Core Principle

Charge based on value and usage, without financially hurting the customer as they grow.

Small companies should be able to start affordably.
Growing companies should pay more because they use more.
Larger companies should pay enough to support heavier usage, support, storage, AI, and infrastructure.

## Pricing Goals

1. Help SLAI become self-sustaining
2. Cover hosting, Firebase, Stripe, AI tools, development tools, and support
3. Keep pricing simple enough for cleaning business owners to understand
4. Avoid hidden fees
5. Support future hiring and growth
6. Encourage long-term customer retention

## Suggested Pricing Model

### Starter

Best for owner-operators and very small teams.

Includes:

- Customer management
- Estimates
- Scheduling
- Basic job tracking
- Invoicing/payment tracking
- Limited employee access
- Basic reports

Target customer:

- 1 to 3 users
- Small cleaning business
- Early-stage company

### Growth

Best for active cleaning businesses with employees.

Includes:

- Everything in Starter
- Employee app
- Training assignments
- Checklists
- Photo uploads
- Messaging
- More automation
- Recurring job support
- More reporting

Target customer:

- 4 to 15 users
- Growing cleaning business
- Needs employee coordination

### Professional

Best for larger cleaning companies.

Includes:

- Everything in Growth
- Advanced reporting
- More users
- More storage
- Priority support
- More customization
- More AI assistance

Target customer:

- 15+ users
- Multiple crews
- Higher job volume

### Enterprise / Custom

For larger companies with heavier requirements.

Includes:

- Custom onboarding
- Custom workflows
- Higher usage limits
- Advanced permissions
- Custom support
- Possible white-label options

## Platform Fee

Current planned platform fee:

- 5% platform fee through Stripe Connect

Purpose:

- Support SLAI revenue
- Help cover infrastructure and ongoing development
- Keep monthly subscription pricing more accessible

Important:

The platform fee should be clearly shown to business owners so there are no surprises.

## Transaction Fees

Stripe fees and platform fees should be transparent.

ServicesOS should clearly show:

- Customer payment amount
- Stripe processing fee
- SLAI platform fee
- Net amount to business

## Discounts

ServicesOS should support preset discounts for cleaning business users.

Examples:

- 5% loyal customer discount
- 10% family/friend discount
- 15% promotional discount
- Custom discount with required note

Discounts should be visible on invoices so customers understand the actual service value and the amount discounted.

## Early Beta Pricing

Early beta customers may receive discounted pricing in exchange for feedback.

Possible beta offer:

- Discounted monthly rate
- Founder customer pricing
- Feedback required
- Case study/testimonial optional after successful use

Beta pricing should not be so low that it makes the product feel worthless.

## What Not To Do

Avoid:

- Hidden fees
- Too many pricing tiers
- Charging for every tiny feature
- Punishing customers for growth
- Enterprise complexity too early
- Pricing so low that SLAI cannot support customers properly

## Pricing Validation Questions

Ask beta customers:

1. Does the price feel fair for the time saved?
2. What software would ServicesOS replace for you?
3. What would you expect to pay monthly?
4. Would you prefer lower subscription + platform fee or higher subscription + lower platform fee?
5. What pricing would feel too expensive?
6. What pricing would feel suspiciously cheap?

## Long-Term Pricing Vision

ServicesOS should grow with the customer.

The customer should feel:

> ServicesOS helped my business grow, and the pricing grew fairly with me.

## Success Signal

Pricing is working when customers say:

> This saves me more time and stress than it costs me.
