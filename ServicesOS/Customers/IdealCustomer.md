# ServicesOS Ideal Customer

## Purpose

This document defines the best early customers for ServicesOS so product decisions, pricing, demos, and beta testing stay focused.

## Primary Ideal Customer

The best early customer is a small-to-growing cleaning business that is already doing real jobs, has repeat customers, and feels pain from managing work manually or across too many disconnected tools.

## Best-Fit Business Profile

- Residential cleaning company
- 1 to 15 employees or cleaners
- Owner still involved in scheduling, quoting, customer communication, and payments
- Uses spreadsheets, paper notes, texts, Facebook messages, QuickBooks, Square, Stripe, or several disconnected apps
- Wants to look more professional without paying enterprise software prices
- Has repeat clients or wants to grow recurring service revenue
- Needs better employee coordination
- Values simple workflows over complicated software

## Strong Early Fit

A business is a strong fit if they struggle with:

- Losing customer information
- Forgetting notes about pets, access instructions, or special requests
- Manually creating estimates
- Manually scheduling jobs
- Tracking deposits and final payments
- Training new cleaners consistently
- Knowing what employees completed at a job
- Sending professional invoices or receipts
- Keeping family/friend discounts visible and fair
- Managing recurring cleanings

## Weak Early Fit

Avoid these customers at first:

- Large enterprise cleaning companies with complex custom requirements
- Businesses needing payroll automation immediately
- Businesses needing heavy inventory automation immediately
- Customers demanding advanced AI routing before the core workflow is stable
- Companies unwilling to test and provide feedback
- Companies that only want the cheapest possible tool and do not value support

## Early Buyer Persona

### Owner-Operator

This person runs the business, handles customer messages, schedules jobs, creates estimates, and often still cleans or helps clean.

They care about:

- Saving time
- Looking professional
- Getting paid faster
- Avoiding missed jobs
- Keeping customers happy
- Not overpaying for software
- Reducing chaos

## Secondary User Personas

### Office/Admin User

Handles scheduling, customer records, invoices, payments, and employee assignments.

Needs:

- Fast customer lookup
- Clear scheduling
- Simple payment tracking
- Easy communication
- Fewer duplicate entries

### Cleaner/Employee

Uses the mobile employee app.

Needs:

- Today’s jobs
- Address and access notes
- Job checklist
- Customer/pet notes
- Training
- Photo upload
- Messaging office/manager
- Simple clock/time tracking

## Positioning Statement

ServicesOS is built for cleaning businesses that are tired of juggling spreadsheets, texts, payment apps, and disconnected subscriptions. It gives owners, admins, and cleaners one connected system for customers, estimates, scheduling, jobs, training, payments, and growth.

## Early Customer Qualification Questions

Ask potential customers:

1. How do you currently manage customer information?
2. How do you create estimates?
3. How do you schedule jobs?
4. How do cleaners know what to do at each job?
5. How do you collect deposits and final payments?
6. What software are you currently paying for?
7. What is the most frustrating part of running the business?
8. What causes the most mistakes or wasted time?
9. How many recurring customers do you have?
10. Would you be willing to beta test a cleaning business platform at a discounted early rate?

## First Customer Goal

The first customer should not be perfect. The first customer should be useful.

Best first customer:

- Gives honest feedback
- Actually uses the system
- Finds bugs
- Explains confusing workflows
- Helps validate pricing
- Helps prove ServicesOS can support real cleaning work

## Success Signal

ServicesOS is solving the right problem when the owner says:

> I can run my cleaning business with fewer tools, fewer missed details, and less stress.
