# ServicesOS Founder Access Qualification Scorecard

Project: Stellar Logic AI / ServicesOS  
Status: Active after wife beta blockers are fixed  
Priority: Lead qualification before demos/onboarding  
Last Updated: 2026-06-28

## Purpose

This document defines how to qualify ServicesOS Founder Access prospects before spending Jamie’s time on live demos or onboarding.

Core rule:

```text
Do not demo everyone.
Qualify first.
Only onboard serious fits.
```

## Must-Have Qualifications

A prospect should have most of these:

- recurring cleaning jobs
- real customer/job volume
- admin pain
- willingness to use software
- willingness to give feedback
- ability to pay $39–$49/month if useful
- workflow that matches ServicesOS V1

## Good Signs

Good signs include:

- has weekly/biweekly/monthly recurring clients
- has 1–5 helpers or is adding first helper
- keeps job details in texts, calendars, paper, spreadsheets, or payment apps
- struggles with helper accountability
- uses or wants checklists/photos/notes
- has move-out, real-estate prep, or turnover work
- asks practical workflow questions
- says “I need something simpler”
- accepts that Founder Access is early
- is willing to give feedback

## Warning Signs

Warning signs include:

- “I just need free software”
- “Can it replace QuickBooks?”
- “Can it do payroll?”
- “Can it text all my customers automatically?”
- “Can you customize it for my exact process?”
- has no recurring jobs
- avoids feedback calls
- expects enterprise maturity
- already loves a mature platform
- refuses to pay after trial

## Automatic Disqualifiers

Disqualify early if they say:

- “I just want something free.”
- “I need payroll before anything else.”
- “I need it to replace QuickBooks.”
- “I need full SMS automation.”
- “I need custom features for my exact process.”
- “I already use Jobber/ZenMaid/Launch27 and love it.”
- “I do not have recurring clients.”
- “I do not have time to give feedback.”
- “I will only use it if it does everything.”

## Scorecard

Score each category from 0–3.

Maximum score: 27.

| Category | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Recurring jobs | none | occasional | monthly | weekly/biweekly |
| Helpers | none, no plans | maybe later | 1 helper | 2–5 helpers |
| Admin pain | low | mild | clear | painful/urgent |
| Tool chaos | organized already | one tool | multiple tools | texts/calendar/spreadsheets/payment apps |
| Software openness | resistant | unsure | willing | actively looking |
| Feedback willingness | no | maybe | yes | excited to help shape it |
| Ability to pay | no | maybe $10–$20 | $29–$39 | $49+ realistic |
| V1 fit | poor | partial | good | very strong |
| Urgency | none | someday | this month | right now |

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Decision |
|---:|---|
| 23–27 | Strong Founder Access candidate. |
| 18–22 | Worth discovery/demo. |
| 13–17 | Interview only, do not onboard yet. |
| Below 13 | Not worth Jamie’s time right now. |

## Pre-Demo Questions

Ask before giving a live demo:

1. How many recurring cleaning clients do you have?
2. How do you track schedules now?
3. How do you keep customer/job notes now?
4. Do you have helpers or plan to?
5. How do helpers know what to do at each job?
6. Do you use checklists or before/after photos?
7. How do you track who paid, deposits, or final payment?
8. What is the most annoying admin task each week?
9. Have you tried software before?
10. Would $39–$49/month be realistic if this saved time and reduced missed details?

## Listen For

Listen for:

- repeated jobs
- helper confusion
- missed notes
- photo proof needs
- payment tracking pain
- owner doing admin at night
- current workflow spread across many tools

## Too Advanced For ServicesOS V1

They may be too advanced if they need:

- payroll immediately
- automatic SMS campaigns
- deep QuickBooks replacement
- live GPS
- advanced route optimization
- franchise/multi-location support
- complex commercial janitorial contracts
- heavy customization
- enterprise reporting

## Too Small / Too Cheap Right Now

They may be too small or too cheap if:

- no recurring clients
- no helpers and no plans to add helpers
- little/no admin pain
- unwilling to pay anything
- unwilling to spend time learning
- everything already works with memory/texts/calendar

## Worth A Demo If

A prospect is worth a demo if they:

- score 18+
- have recurring jobs
- have admin pain
- understand the workflow problem
- are not demanding excluded features
- can realistically pay after the trial
- are willing to give feedback

## Worth Onboarding If

A prospect is worth onboarding if they:

- completes discovery call
- understands ServicesOS is early
- accepts limitations
- has real jobs to test
- agrees to feedback/check-ins
- agrees to use ServicesOS on at least one real recurring job
- understands post-trial pricing

## Final Rule

```text
A curious lead is not a customer.
A qualified recurring cleaning owner with real workflow pain is the target.
```
