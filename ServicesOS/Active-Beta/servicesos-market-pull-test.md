# ServicesOS Market Pull Test

Project: Stellar Logic AI / ServicesOS  
Status: Active after wife beta blockers are fixed  
Priority: Decide whether cleaning wedge is pulling before building more  
Last Updated: 2026-06-28

## Purpose

This document defines a fair, low-budget market test for ServicesOS after wife beta.

The goal is to avoid two bad outcomes:

1. building ServicesOS forever without customer evidence
2. abandoning ServicesOS before a fair test

Core rule:

```text
Do not keep building features without customer evidence.
Do not abandon the foundation without a fair market test.
```

## Fair Test Size

Before judging the cleaning wedge, Jamie should complete:

```text
50 targeted contacts
15 discovery calls
5 demos
2 Founder Access offers
1 onboarded customer attempt
```

Run this over 60–90 days after wife beta, not forever.

## Acceptable Early Signals

For a zero-budget founder-led test:

- 10–20% response from warm/referral channels is good.
- 5–10% response from cold local outreach is acceptable.
- 30%+ of discovery calls leading to demo is good.
- 20%+ of demos leading to Founder Access offer is good.
- 1 retained recurring user is a major win.

## Market Is Pulling If

Market pull signals:

- owners describe the pain before Jamie does
- they ask to see the demo
- they ask “can I use this on my next job?”
- they accept limited scope
- $39–$49/month does not shock them
- they use it again after week one
- they refer another cleaner
- they complain about specific workflow friction instead of disappearing

## Pitch Is Wrong If

Pitch may be wrong if:

- people do not understand what it does
- they think it is accounting software
- they think it is only a booking website
- they ask about AI/payroll/SMS before job workflow
- they say “sounds cool” but never demo
- they do not understand why it is different from calendar/payment apps

Fix message/channel before adding features.

## Target Is Wrong If

Target may be wrong if prospects:

- have no recurring jobs
- are too small to feel pain
- are too advanced for V1
- already love existing software
- cannot pay anything
- need payroll/accounting/SMS/GPS immediately
- refuse feedback

Fix ICP before adding features.

## Pricing Is Wrong If

Pricing may be wrong if:

- strong-fit prospects love the workflow but hesitate only at price
- multiple serious prospects say $39–$49/month is not realistic
- they want to pay only after a longer proof period
- they need more risk reversal before starting

Possible adjustments:

- keep 30 days free
- extend first paid period at $29/month for a limited time
- keep $49/month founder lock after proof
- do not give unlimited free usage

## Product Is Not Ready If

Product may not be ready if:

- wife beta workflow breaks
- demo flow is confusing
- onboarding takes hours
- field workflow loses data
- payment status is unclear
- helpers cannot use it
- owner cannot see job proof clearly
- customer/estimate/schedule/assignment flow is unreliable

Fix beta blockers before outreach.

## Pivot Wedge If

Consider pivoting the wedge if:

- move-out/turnover cleaners show stronger urgency than regular cleaners
- carpet cleaners show higher willingness to pay
- mobile detailers understand the photo/proof workflow faster
- regular cleaning owners only want free tools
- recurring cleaning workflow is not painful enough
- external cleaning users do not keep using the product after week one

Pivot the wedge, not the whole ServicesOS foundation.

Possible wedge pivots:

```text
residential cleaning
→ move-out / real-estate prep cleaning
→ short-term rental turnover cleaning
→ carpet cleaning
→ mobile detailing
→ window / pressure washing
```

## Do Not Build To Escape Market Feedback

Do not respond to slow traction by building:

- GrowthAI
- SLAIOS
- EducationOS
- RetailOS
- PharmacyOS
- anti-cheat
- FPS Coach
- custom AI models
- full payroll
- advanced routing
- live GPS
- training library
- Tap to Pay

Those may matter later, but not before ServicesOS proves recurring usage.

## Weekly Evidence Review

Track weekly:

- contacts sent
- response count
- discovery calls booked
- demos completed
- applications submitted
- fit scores
- objections
- requested missing features
- willingness to pay
- onboarded users
- real jobs run
- repeat usage
- churn signals
- referrals

## Decision Rules

1. If wife beta fails, fix workflow before outreach.
2. If wife beta works but no one responds, change message/channel before building features.
3. If prospects like it but will not pay, adjust offer/risk/pricing.
4. If 3+ qualified prospects request the same missing V1 feature, consider it.
5. If one prospect asks for a custom feature, do not build it.
6. If no qualified cleaning prospect bites after 50 contacts / 15 calls / 5 demos, test move-out/turnover and carpet cleaning messaging.
7. If one recurring customer keeps using it after first month, keep going.
8. If customers only use it once and disappear, the activation workflow is wrong.
9. If founder support exceeds what $39–$49/month can justify, tighten qualification.
10. No future SLAI projects until ServicesOS proves recurring usage.

## Success Definition

The first win is not traffic, signups, likes, or demo views.

The first win is:

```text
One cleaning owner uses ServicesOS on real recurring jobs,
pays after the first month,
and would be annoyed if you took it away.
```

## Final Rule

```text
Build until testable.
Then test demand.
Only build more when repeated evidence says what to build.
```
