## Purpose

This document defines the low-budget ServicesOS market-entry plan for getting the first real recurring cleaning customer after wife beta.

The goal is not broad SaaS launch.

The goal is:

```text
Get one real recurring cleaning customer to use ServicesOS,
keep using it after the first month,
and start proving ServicesOS can fund its own growth.
```

## Executive Summary

Jamie should not launch ServicesOS broadly after wife beta.

He should run a controlled Founder Access test with a tiny number of qualified cleaning owner-operators.

The cheapest realistic path:

1. Finish wife beta.
2. Fix only beta blockers.
3. Create a simple website plus interactive fake-data demo.
4. Ask warm/local networks for referrals.
5. Interview 10 good-fit cleaning owners.
6. Demo 3–5.
7. Onboard 1 real recurring customer manually.
8. Prove they use it again after the first month.

The first proof is not signups.

The first proof is:

```text
One real cleaning business uses ServicesOS for recurring jobs after the first month without Jamie forcing it.
```

Avoid:

- paid ads
- cold email blasts
- AI-powered messaging
- trying to beat Jobber/ZenMaid/Launch27 feature-for-feature
- onboarding bad-fit customers just because they say yes
- building requested features before repeated evidence
- offering unlimited free support to people who will never pay

## Market Entry Positioning

Best positioning:

```text
Cleaning job workflow software for small owner-operators.
```

This is stronger than “cleaning business operating system” for early outreach because it tells the owner exactly what it helps with.

### One-Sentence Positioning

ServicesOS helps small cleaning businesses manage estimates, schedules, helper assignments, job notes, photos, and payment status without juggling texts, calendars, and spreadsheets.

### Homepage Hero Line

```text
Run cleaning jobs from estimate to payment without losing notes, photos, schedules, or payment status in texts.
```

### Short Pitch

ServicesOS is founder-supported software for small cleaning businesses that need a simpler way to manage customers, estimates, schedules, helpers, checklists, job photos, notes, and payment status.

### 30-Second Pitch

ServicesOS is built for small cleaning owner-operators who are starting to outgrow texts, Google Calendar, paper notes, and payment apps. It helps you take a customer from estimate to scheduled job, assign a helper, collect checklist/photos/notes from the field, review the completed job, and track deposit/final payment status.

### 2-Minute Founder Pitch

ServicesOS started because my wife’s cleaning business needed a simpler way to manage real cleaning workflow. I’m not trying to sell bloated software or claim it replaces everything. The first version is focused on the basics that small cleaning owners deal with every week: customers, estimates, schedules, helper assignments, job checklists, photos, notes, completion proof, and payment status. I’m looking for a few good-fit cleaning businesses to join Founder Access, use it on real recurring jobs, and tell me what actually helps or gets in the way.

## Founder Access Offer

Recommended offer:

```text
Founder Access:
30 days free
→ $39/month for first 3 months
→ $49/month founder lock for 12 months while active
```

Why this is best:

- Low-risk for the first customer.
- Filters out total freebie hunters.
- Gets Jamie revenue quickly.
- Keeps pricing below many mature tools.
- Avoids giving away 60+ days to people who are only curious.
- Makes continued usage after month one the real proof.

### Who Qualifies

- Residential cleaning owner-operator.
- 1–5 helpers or planning to add helpers.
- Has recurring weekly/biweekly/monthly clients.
- Uses texts, paper, Google Calendar, spreadsheets, Square, QuickBooks, Wave, Cash App, Venmo, or memory.
- Has admin pain around notes, photos, schedules, estimates, helper accountability, or payment status.
- Willing to give feedback during first month.

### Who Does Not Qualify

- Already happy with Jobber, ZenMaid, Launch27, BookingKoala, or Housecall Pro.
- Needs payroll immediately.
- Needs advanced SMS automation immediately.
- Needs full QuickBooks replacement.
- Needs route optimization or live GPS right now.
- Wants custom software.
- Has no recurring jobs.
- Refuses feedback.
- Only wants free software.

### What They Get

- Founder-led onboarding.
- Manual customer/job import help.
- Core ServicesOS workflow.
- Founder support during first month.
- Direct feedback channel.
- Founder price lock for 12 months if they continue.

### What They Agree To

- Use it on real jobs.
- Give feedback during first month.
- Report blockers honestly.
- Accept early limitations.
- Not expect custom development for one business.
- Pay after the first free month if it is useful.

2. Create estimate.
3. Schedule job.
4. Assign helper.
5. Helper opens job.
6. Helper completes checklist.
7. Helper adds notes/issues.
8. Helper adds fake/photo placeholders.
9. Helper completes job.
10. Owner reviews completed job.
11. Owner sees payment status.

Do not show:

- real Stripe
- real payments
- real uploads
- real SMS
- real emails
- real customer data
- advanced reports
- payroll
- AI
- route optimization
- integrations
- unfinished features

Demo should be public, but Founder Access application should be separate.

Use localStorage only so demo users do not cost money.

Onboarding process:

- real customer/job data entered
- at least one real job scheduled
- helper assigned
- checklist/photos/notes used
- owner reviews completed job
- payment status tracked
- customer uses it again next week

### Days 61–90

Goal:

```text
Move from one customer to first five or decide what needs changing.
```

Actions:

- Continue cleaning-first outreach.

- Build only repeated pain.
- Improve onboarding.
- Improve demo.
- Improve field workflow.

Signals to keep going:

- one customer keeps using it after first month
- 3+ prospects understand the pain

- cleaners like the idea but will not pay

- carpet cleaners/mobile detailers show stronger payment willingness
- first cleaning users only want free software
- the pitch resonates better with job-proof/photo-heavy work

## Market Pull Test

Before judging the cleaning wedge, complete:

- 50 targeted contacts
- 15 discovery calls
- 5 demos
- 2 Founder Access offers
- 1 onboarded customer attempt

Run this over 60–90 days after wife beta, not forever.

Market is pulling if:

- owners describe the pain before Jamie does
- they ask to see the demo
- they ask “can I use this on my next job?”
- they accept limited scope
- $39–$49/month does not shock them
- they use it again after week one
- they refer another cleaner

## Decision Rules

1. If wife beta fails, fix workflow before outreach.
2. If wife beta works but no one responds, change message/channel before building features.
3. If prospects like it but will not pay, adjust offer/risk/pricing.
4. If 3+ qualified prospects request the same missing V1 feature, consider it.
5. If one prospect asks for a custom feature, do not build it.
6. If no qualified cleaning prospect bites after 50 contacts / 15 calls / 5 demos, test move-out/turnover and carpet cleaning messaging.
7. If one recurring customer keeps using it after first month, keep going.
8. If customers only use it once and disappear, the activation workflow is wrong.
9. If founder support exceeds what $39–$49/month can justify, tighten qualification.
10. No GrowthAI, SLAIOS, EducationOS, RetailOS, PharmacyOS, FPS Coach, anti-cheat, or FutureAI work until ServicesOS proves recurring usage.
