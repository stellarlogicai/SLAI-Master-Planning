Task 3: Slide Content
Slide 1: Why Incident Reporting Matters
Content

Incident reporting helps:

Protect employees
Protect customers
Protect the company
Support insurance claims
Improve operations

Accurate reporting creates accountability and transparency.

Key Takeaway
If something happens, document it.
Slide 2: What Is A Reportable Incident?
Content

Examples include:

Property damage
Employee injury
Equipment failure
Customer complaint
Safety concern
Access issue

When in doubt, report it.

Key Takeaway
Reporting is always better than guessing.
Slide 3: Level 1 — Information
Content

Examples:

Missing supplies
Customer note
Minor observations
Low-priority concerns

Usually does not require immediate action.

Key Takeaway
Information reports improve visibility.
Slide 4: Level 2 — Operational Issues
Content

Examples:

Locked out
Running late
Equipment malfunction
Scheduling conflict

Management notification may be required.

Key Takeaway
Operational issues can affect service quality.
Slide 5: Level 3 — Property Damage
Content

Examples:

Broken decoration
Damaged furniture
Scratched flooring
Water damage

Immediate reporting is required.

Key Takeaway
Property damage must be reported immediately.
Slide 6: Level 4 — Safety Incidents
Content

Examples:

Employee injury
Chemical exposure
Slip and fall
Unsafe environment

Safety takes priority over job completion.

Key Takeaway
Protect people before protecting schedules.
Slide 7: Level 5 — Emergencies
Content

Examples:

Medical emergency
Fire
Gas leak
Police involvement
Major property damage

Emergency procedures take priority.

Key Takeaway
Emergency response comes first.
Slide 8: Capturing Accurate Information
Content

Document:

Date
Time
Location
People involved
What happened

Record facts only.

Key Takeaway
Report facts, not assumptions.
Slide 9: Photo Documentation Standards
Content

Photos should:

Be clear
Be accurate
Show relevant details
Be taken immediately when safe

Do not edit or alter photos.

Key Takeaway
Photos provide objective evidence.
Slide 10: Writing Effective Notes
Content

Good notes are:

Clear
Objective
Accurate
Professional

Avoid emotional language.

Example
Incorrect:
"The customer was rude and caused problems."

Correct:
"Customer expressed concern regarding service completion."
Key Takeaway
Professional notes improve credibility.
Slide 11: When To Notify Management
Content

Always notify management for:

Property damage
Injuries
Customer disputes
Safety concerns
Emergencies
Key Takeaway
Escalate serious incidents immediately.
Slide 12: Emergency Procedures
Content

In emergencies:

Ensure safety
Contact emergency services if needed
Notify management
Document when safe
Key Takeaway
Safety comes before reporting.
Slide 13: Customer Communication
Content

Remain:

Professional
Calm
Respectful

Never:

Admit fault
Assign blame
Argue

Follow company communication policies.

Key Takeaway
Professional communication protects everyone.
Slide 14: Submitting Reports In The App
Content

Incident reports may include:

Severity level

Notes

Attachments

Submit reports promptly.

Key Takeaway
Timely reporting improves response times.

Content

Report incidents promptly

Key Takeaway

Question 1

Why is incident reporting important?

A. It protects employees

B. It protects customers

C. It protects the company

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 2

What should an employee do if unsure whether an incident should be reported?

A. Ignore it

B. Wait until next week

C. Report it

D. Ask the customer

Correct Answer: C

Question 3

Which is an example of a Level 1 incident?

A. Medical emergency

B. Missing supplies

C. Broken window

D. Employee injury

Correct Answer: B

Question 4

Which is an example of a Level 2 incident?

A. Fire

B. Gas leak

C. Equipment malfunction

D. Serious injury

Correct Answer: C

Question 5

Which incident requires immediate reporting?

A. Property damage

B. Minor supply shortage

C. Customer preference note

D. General observation

Correct Answer: A

Question 6

What is an example of a Level 4 incident?

A. Missing mop

B. Schedule change

C. Employee injury

D. Customer feedback

Correct Answer: C

Question 7

What is an example of a Level 5 incident?

A. Medical emergency

B. Low inventory

C. Late arrival

D. Broken vacuum

Correct Answer: A

Question 8

Which information should be included in a report?

A. Date

B. Time

C. Location

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 9

Incident reports should contain:

A. Assumptions

B. Opinions

C. Facts

D. Rumors

Correct Answer: C

Question 10

Photos used in incident reports should be:

A. Edited

B. Accurate and clear

C. Filtered

D. Cropped to hide details

Correct Answer: B

Question 11

Which note is written professionally?

A. "Customer was rude."

B. "Customer was angry for no reason."

C. "Customer expressed concern regarding service completion."

D. "Customer caused problems."

Correct Answer: C

Question 12

Which issue should always be escalated?

A. Property damage

B. Safety concerns

C. Injuries

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 13

In an emergency, what comes first?

A. Documentation

B. Cleaning completion

C. Safety

D. Customer survey

Correct Answer: C

Question 14

What should employees avoid during incident discussions?

A. Professional communication

B. Remaining calm

C. Assigning blame

D. Reporting facts

Correct Answer: C

Question 15

What is the best reporting practice?

A. Report immediately and accurately

B. Wait until the end of the week

C. Guess missing information

D. Skip photo documentation

Correct Answer: A
