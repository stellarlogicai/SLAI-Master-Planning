Task 3: Slide Content
Slide 1: Floors & Surfaces Overview
Content

Floors and surfaces represent some of the largest visible areas in a home.

Customers immediately notice:

Dirt
Streaks
Scratches
Damage
Missed debris

Proper cleaning protects both appearance and value.

Key Takeaway
Clean floors improve the appearance of the entire home.
Slide 2: Why Surface Identification Matters
Content

Not all surfaces can be cleaned the same way.

Using the wrong:

Chemical
Tool
Mop
Cleaning process

can cause expensive damage.

Key Takeaway
Always identify the surface before cleaning.
Slide 3: Hardwood Floors
Content

Hardwood requires special care.

Always:

Use approved hardwood products
Use minimal moisture
Follow grain direction when possible

Never:

Saturate hardwood
Use excessive water
Use harsh chemicals
Key Takeaway
Water is one of hardwood's biggest enemies.
Slide 4: Laminate Flooring
Content

Laminate can be damaged by excess moisture.

Always:

Use approved cleaners
Use lightly damp mops

Avoid:

Standing water
Excessive scrubbing
Key Takeaway
Less moisture equals less risk.
Slide 5: Vinyl & Luxury Vinyl Plank
Content

Vinyl surfaces are durable but still require care.

Use:

Approved neutral cleaners
Proper mopping procedures

Avoid:

Abrasive pads
Harsh chemicals
Key Takeaway
Durable does not mean indestructible.
Slide 6: Tile & Grout
Content

Focus on:

Surface cleanliness
Grout appearance
Corner buildup

Pay attention to:

Bathroom tile
Kitchen tile
Entryways
Key Takeaway
Clean grout significantly improves appearance.
Slide 7: Natural Stone Surfaces
Content

Examples:

Marble
Granite
Slate
Travertine

Only use approved stone-safe products.

Never use acidic cleaners unless specifically approved.

Key Takeaway
Natural stone requires specialized care.
Slide 8: Carpet & Area Rugs
Content

Inspect for:

Stains
Debris
Wear patterns

Vacuum thoroughly and follow company procedures for spot treatment.

Key Takeaway
Aggressive cleaning can damage carpet fibers.
Slide 9: Vacuuming Standards
Content

Vacuum:

Along edges
Under accessible furniture
High-traffic areas
Stairs

Use overlapping passes for complete coverage.

Key Takeaway
Slow, thorough vacuuming produces better results.
Slide 10: Mopping Standards
Content

Before mopping:

Remove debris
Sweep or vacuum

During mopping:

Use correct solution
Avoid oversaturation
Change dirty water when necessary
Key Takeaway
Clean water produces clean floors.
Slide 11: Spot Cleaning Procedures
Content

When addressing stains:

Identify surface
Identify stain
Use approved treatment
Test when required
Follow company procedures
Key Takeaway
Never guess when treating stains.
Slide 12: Stair Cleaning Procedures
Content

Clean:

Treads
Risers
Railings
Corners

Exercise caution while carrying equipment.

Key Takeaway
Safety is critical when working on stairs.
Slide 13: Preventing Surface Damage
Content

Always:

Follow approved procedures
Use approved products
Test unfamiliar surfaces when required

Report concerns immediately.

Key Takeaway
Preventing damage is more important than speed.
Slide 14: Common Mistakes
Content

Examples:

Too much water on hardwood
Wrong chemical on stone
Using abrasive tools
Ignoring manufacturer recommendations

Most damage comes from preventable mistakes.

Key Takeaway
Follow procedures, not assumptions.
Slide 15: Final Inspection & Summary
Content

Before completion:

Inspect floors
Check corners
Verify streak-free appearance
Confirm debris removal
Document concerns
Key Takeaway
Inspect every floor before moving on.

Question 1

Why is surface identification important?

A. It helps determine the correct cleaning method

B. It increases cleaning speed

C. It reduces documentation

D. It changes schedules

Correct Answer: A

Question 2

What is one of the biggest risks to hardwood floors?

A. Dust

B. Excess water

C. Vacuuming

D. Dry mopping

Correct Answer: B

Question 3

When cleaning laminate flooring, employees should:

A. Use standing water

B. Use lightly damp cleaning methods

C. Flood the surface

D. Use abrasive pads

Correct Answer: B

Question 4

Which surface requires specialized cleaning products?

A. Natural stone

B. Carpet

C. Vinyl

D. Laminate

Correct Answer: A

Question 5

Which is considered natural stone?

A. Marble

B. Granite

C. Travertine

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 6

What should be done before mopping?

A. Sweep or vacuum debris

B. Add extra cleaner

C. Wet the floor heavily

D. Move randomly through rooms

Correct Answer: A

Question 7

Why should dirty mop water be changed?

A. To maintain cleaning effectiveness

B. To increase drying time

C. To save water

D. To reduce inspections

Correct Answer: A

Question 8

When treating stains, employees should:

A. Guess the correct chemical

B. Identify the surface first

C. Use the strongest product available

D. Ignore manufacturer guidance

Correct Answer: B

Question 9

Which area should be vacuumed carefully?

A. Edges

B. High-traffic areas

C. Under accessible furniture

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 10

What is a common cause of surface damage?

A. Following procedures

B. Using approved products

C. Using the wrong chemical

D. Performing inspections

Correct Answer: C
