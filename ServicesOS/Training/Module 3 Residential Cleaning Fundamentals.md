Slide 1: Residential Cleaning Fundamentals
Content

Residential cleaning is the foundation of our business.

Every customer expects:

Consistent results
Attention to detail
Professional service
Reliable quality

Following company procedures helps ensure every home receives the same high standard of care.

Key Takeaway
Consistency creates customer trust.
Slide 2: What Defines Quality Cleaning
Content

Quality cleaning means:

Surfaces are cleaned properly
Trash is removed
Floors are maintained
Bathrooms are sanitized
Kitchens are cleaned safely
Customer expectations are met

Quality is measured by results, not speed.

Key Takeaway
Do the job right the first time.
Slide 3: Working Top To Bottom
Content

Always clean from higher surfaces to lower surfaces.

Example:

Ceiling Fans
↓
Shelves
↓
Counters
↓
Furniture
↓
Floors

This prevents dust and debris from falling onto already-cleaned surfaces.

Key Takeaway
Top-to-bottom cleaning reduces rework.
Slide 4: Working Clean To Dirty
Content

Clean the least dirty areas first.

Example:

Living Room
↓
Bedroom
↓
Kitchen
↓
Bathroom

This reduces contamination and improves efficiency.

Key Takeaway
Avoid spreading dirt and bacteria.
Slide 5: Room-By-Room Workflow
Content

Complete one room before moving to the next.

Typical workflow:

Living Areas
↓
Bedrooms
↓
Kitchen
↓
Bathrooms

Avoid jumping between rooms.

Key Takeaway
Stay organized and systematic.
Slide 6: Preventing Cross Contamination
Content

Cross contamination occurs when dirt, bacteria, or chemicals are transferred between areas.

Examples:

Using bathroom cloths in kitchens
Using dirty tools in clean areas

Always use proper cleaning procedures.

Key Takeaway
Keep cleaning tools separated by purpose.
Slide 7: Color-Coded Cloths & Tools
Content

Many companies use color coding:

Blue = General Surfaces

Yellow = Bathrooms

Green = Kitchens

Red = High-Risk Areas

Follow your company's procedures.

Key Takeaway
Color coding helps prevent contamination.

Efficiency should never reduce quality.

Question 1

What is one of the primary goals of residential cleaning?

A. Finish as quickly as possible

B. Deliver consistent quality service

C. Skip unnecessary tasks

D. Use as many chemicals as possible

Correct Answer: B

Question 2

What does "top-to-bottom cleaning" mean?

A. Start with floors

B. Clean lower surfaces first

C. Clean higher surfaces before lower surfaces

D. Clean rooms randomly

Correct Answer: C

Question 3

Why is top-to-bottom cleaning important?

A. It prevents dust from falling onto cleaned areas

B. It saves chemical costs

C. It reduces travel time

D. It increases paperwork

Correct Answer: A

Question 4

What does "clean-to-dirty" cleaning mean?

A. Clean the dirtiest room first

B. Clean the least dirty areas before heavily soiled areas

C. Clean only bathrooms

D. Clean rooms in alphabetical order

Correct Answer: B

Question 5

What is the recommended room workflow?

A. Jump between rooms

B. Complete one room before moving to the next

C. Clean floors first everywhere

D. Clean bathrooms last only

Correct Answer: B

Question 6

What is cross contamination?

A. Cleaning multiple rooms

B. Using multiple products

C. Transferring dirt, bacteria, or chemicals between areas

D. Using a vacuum

Correct Answer: C

Question 7

Which action may cause cross contamination?

A. Using separate cloths for kitchens and bathrooms

B. Following company procedures

C. Using a bathroom cloth in the kitchen

D. Replacing dirty supplies

Correct Answer: C

Question 8

Why do some companies use color-coded cloths?

A. Decoration

B. Prevent cross contamination

C. Marketing

D. Inventory counting

Correct Answer: B

Question 9

Which practice improves efficiency?

A. Repeatedly returning to the supply vehicle

B. Working without a checklist

C. Preparing supplies before starting

D. Skipping inspections

Correct Answer: C

Question 10

Before beginning a job, employees should:

A. Check equipment and supplies

B. Skip preparation

C. Wait for customer instructions

D. Begin immediately

Correct Answer: A

Question 11

What is an example of attention to detail?

A. Ignoring corners

B. Checking for fingerprints and dust

C. Leaving supplies behind

Question 12

Which area is commonly overlooked?

A. Baseboards

Question 13

What should happen before leaving a job?

A. Immediate departure

Question 14

Why are photos and notes important?

A. They verify work completed

C. They help resolve disputes
