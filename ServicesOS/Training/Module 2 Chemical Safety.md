Content

Cleaning chemicals are valuable tools when used properly.

Improper use can result in:

Injuries
Respiratory problems
Property damage
Customer complaints
Environmental hazards

Every employee is responsible for chemical safety.

Key Takeaway
Always follow approved chemical procedures.
Slide 2: Why Chemical Safety Matters
Content

Chemical accidents can impact:

Employees
Customers
Pets
Property
The company

Safety protects everyone.

Key Takeaway
Safety comes before speed.
Slide 3: Common Cleaning Chemicals
Content

Examples include:

Glass cleaners
Disinfectants
Degreasers
Bathroom cleaners
Floor cleaners
Multi-surface cleaners

Only use company-approved products.

Key Takeaway
Use only approved chemicals.
Slide 4: Reading Labels
Content

Always review:

Product name
Usage instructions
Safety warnings
PPE requirements
First aid information

Labels contain critical safety information.

Key Takeaway
Read labels before use.
Slide 5: Warning Symbols & Hazard Indicators
Content

Common hazards include:

Corrosive materials
Eye irritants
Skin irritants
Respiratory hazards

Pay attention to warning symbols and instructions.

Key Takeaway
Warnings exist to prevent injuries.
Slide 6: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Content

Examples:

Gloves
Safety glasses
Masks
Protective clothing

PPE reduces exposure risk.

Key Takeaway
Use PPE whenever required.
Slide 7: When PPE Must Be Used
Content

Use PPE when:

Handling concentrated chemicals
Using disinfectants
Cleaning heavily contaminated areas
Working with products that require protection
Key Takeaway
When in doubt, wear PPE.
Slide 8: Safe Chemical Handling
Content

Always:

Follow instructions
Measure properly
Avoid splashing
Keep containers closed

Never:

Guess dilution rates
Use unmarked containers
Key Takeaway
Handle chemicals carefully and deliberately.
Slide 9: Chemical Storage Procedures
Content

Store chemicals:

In labeled containers
Away from children and pets
In designated storage areas
Away from heat sources
Key Takeaway
Proper storage prevents accidents.
Slide 10: Transporting Chemicals Safely
Content

When transporting chemicals:

Secure containers
Keep lids closed
Prevent tipping
Separate incompatible products
Key Takeaway
Transport chemicals safely.
Slide 11: Never Mix Chemicals
Content

Some chemical combinations can create toxic gases.

Examples:

Bleach + Ammonia
Bleach + Certain Acid-Based Cleaners

Only use products as directed.

Key Takeaway
Never mix chemicals unless specifically approved.
Slide 12: Chemical Spill Response
Content

If a spill occurs:

Stop work
Secure the area
Follow cleanup procedures
Notify management
Document the incident
Key Takeaway
Respond quickly and safely.
Slide 13: Chemical Exposure Response
Content

If exposure occurs:

Follow label first-aid instructions
Seek medical attention if necessary
Notify management immediately
Document the incident
Key Takeaway
Report exposure immediately.
Slide 14: Incident Reporting Procedures
Content

Report:

Spills
Exposure incidents
Damaged containers
Missing labels
Customer concerns

Use the employee app whenever possible.

Key Takeaway
Timely reporting prevents larger problems.
Slide 15: Safety Responsibilities & Summary
Content

Every employee is responsible for:

Safe handling
PPE usage
Proper storage
Incident reporting

Safety is a daily responsibility.

Key Takeaway
Everyone contributes to a safe workplace.

Question 1

What should you do before using any cleaning chemical?

A. Use it immediately

B. Read the product label

C. Mix it with another cleaner

D. Ask a customer

Correct Answer: B

Question 2

Which of the following is an approved source of safety information?

A. Product label

B. Safety instructions

C. Company procedures

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 3

What is PPE?

A. Professional Property Equipment

B. Personal Protective Equipment

C. Product Protection Equipment

D. Personal Productivity Evaluation

Correct Answer: B

Question 4

Which is considered PPE?

A. Gloves

B. Safety glasses

C. Face mask

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 5

When should PPE be worn?

A. Only when customers are present

B. Only during inspections

C. Whenever required by the product or company policy

D. Never

Correct Answer: C

Question 6

What should you do if a chemical container loses its label?

A. Continue using it

B. Guess what it contains

C. Report it immediately

D. Throw it into regular trash

Correct Answer: C

Question 7

Where should chemicals be stored?

A. Any available location

B. Properly labeled designated storage areas

C. Employee vehicles permanently

D. Customer cabinets

Correct Answer: B

Question 8

Which statement is true?

A. Mixing chemicals improves cleaning performance

B. Mixing chemicals is safe if done carefully

C. Chemicals should only be used as directed

D. All chemicals are compatible

Correct Answer: C

Question 9

Which combination is dangerous?

A. Water and glass cleaner

B. Bleach and ammonia

C. Floor cleaner and mop water

D. Approved dilution mixtures

Correct Answer: B

Question 10

If a chemical spill occurs, what should be your first action?

A. Ignore it

B. Continue cleaning

C. Stop work and secure the area

D. Leave immediately

Correct Answer: C

Question 11

What should you do if chemical exposure occurs?

A. Hide the incident

B. Follow first aid instructions and report it

C. Finish the job first

D. Wait until tomorrow

Correct Answer: B

Question 12

Why are chemical labels important?

A. They provide safety information

B. They provide usage instructions

C. They identify hazards

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 13

What should be reported?

A. Chemical spills

B. Exposure incidents

C. Missing labels

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 14

Who is responsible for workplace safety?

A. Management only

B. Crew leaders only

C. Safety inspectors only

D. Everyone

Correct Answer: D

Question 15

What is the safest approach when unsure about a chemical?

A. Use it anyway

B. Mix it with another product

C. Ask for guidance before using it

D. Experiment

Correct Answer: C
