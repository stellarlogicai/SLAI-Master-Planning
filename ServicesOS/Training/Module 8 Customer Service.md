Task 3: Slide Content
Slide 1: Customer Service Matters
Content

Customers remember how they were treated.

Great customer service leads to:

Repeat business
Positive reviews
Referrals
Customer loyalty

Poor customer service can lose a customer even when cleaning quality is excellent.

Key Takeaway
Customer service is part of the job.
Slide 2: You Represent The Company
Content

Every interaction shapes the customer's perception.

Customers judge:

Professionalism
Appearance
Communication
Reliability

You represent the company whenever you are on a job.

Key Takeaway
You are the face of the company.
Slide 3: Professional Communication
Content

Always:

Be respectful
Speak clearly
Remain professional
Stay positive

Avoid:

Arguments
Complaints
Negative comments
Unprofessional language
Key Takeaway
Professional communication builds trust.
Slide 4: Greeting Customers
Content

When appropriate:

Introduce yourself
Greet the customer politely
Confirm service details

Example:

Good morning, I'm Sarah with ABC Cleaning.
We're here for your scheduled cleaning today.
Key Takeaway
A positive first impression matters.
Slide 5: Listening Skills
Content

Customers want to feel heard.

Practice:

Active listening
Asking clarifying questions
Confirming understanding
Key Takeaway
Listen before responding.
Slide 6: Managing Expectations
Content

Communicate clearly.

Never:

Promise unauthorized work
Guarantee services outside scope

If uncertain:

Contact management
Key Takeaway
Set realistic expectations.
Slide 7: Respecting Customer Property
Content

Always:

Treat property carefully
Respect belongings
Avoid unnecessary movement of items

Report accidental damage immediately.

Key Takeaway
Treat every home with respect.
Slide 8: Privacy & Confidentiality
Content

Never:

Share customer information
Discuss customer homes
Take unauthorized photos

Customer privacy is essential.

Key Takeaway
Confidentiality protects trust.
Slide 9: Working Around Pets
Content

Pets may be:

Friendly
Nervous
Protective

Follow customer instructions.

Never:

Feed pets
Discipline pets
Open exterior doors carelessly
Key Takeaway
Pet safety matters.
Slide 10: Working Around Children
Content

Always:

Remain professional
Be respectful
Follow company procedures

Never:

Babysit
Supervise children
Enter inappropriate conversations
Key Takeaway
Maintain professional boundaries.
Slide 11: Customer Complaints
Content

If a customer complains:

Listen calmly
Avoid arguing
Gather information
Notify management
Key Takeaway
Listen first. Escalate appropriately.
Slide 12: Scope Change Requests
Content

Customers may request additional work.

Examples:

Extra room
Appliance cleaning
Deep cleaning requests

Do not approve additional work without company authorization.

Key Takeaway
Additional work requires approval.
Slide 13: When To Escalate
Content

Escalate:

Complaints
Scope changes
Safety concerns
Damage incidents
Customer disputes
Key Takeaway
Know when to involve management.
Slide 14: Creating Five-Star Experiences
Content

Customers appreciate:

Professionalism
Reliability
Communication
Attention to detail

Small actions create memorable experiences.

Key Takeaway
Every interaction affects customer retention.
Slide 15: Summary & Key Takeaways
Content

Remember:

Be professional
Communicate clearly
Respect customers
Protect privacy
Escalate when necessary

Customer service is a competitive advantage.

Key Takeaway
Professional service creates loyal customers.

Question 1

What is one of the primary goals of customer service?

A. Finish jobs faster

B. Build customer trust and satisfaction

C. Reduce cleaning quality

D. Avoid communication

Correct Answer: B

Question 2

Who represents the company while on a job?

A. Management only

B. Sales staff only

C. Every employee

D. Customers

Correct Answer: C

Question 3

Professional communication includes:

A. Respectful language

B. Clear communication

C. Positive interactions

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 4

What should you do when arriving at a customer's home?

A. Ignore the customer

B. Politely greet and introduce yourself

C. Start cleaning immediately

D. Wait silently

Correct Answer: B

Question 5

What is active listening?

A. Interrupting frequently

B. Listening carefully and confirming understanding

C. Ignoring customer concerns

D. Avoiding questions

Correct Answer: B

Question 6

If you are unsure about a customer request, you should:

A. Promise completion

B. Contact management

C. Guess

D. Refuse service

Correct Answer: B

Question 7

A. Reorganized

B. Handled carefully and respectfully

C. Moved freely

D. Ignored

Correct Answer: B

Question 8

Customer information should be:

A. Shared with friends

C. Kept confidential

D. Discussed publicly
