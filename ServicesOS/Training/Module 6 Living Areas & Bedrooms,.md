Task 3: Slide Content
Slide 1: Living Areas & Bedrooms Standards
Content

Living rooms and bedrooms represent comfort, organization, and cleanliness.

Customers expect:

Dust-free surfaces
Organized appearance
Clean floors
Properly made beds
Fresh presentation

These rooms are often where customers spend the most time.

Key Takeaway
Presentation is just as important as cleanliness.
Slide 2: Why Presentation Matters
Content

Customers often judge quality based on:

Visual appearance
Organization
Dust levels
Floor condition
Bed appearance

A room should feel refreshed when cleaning is complete.

Key Takeaway
Customers notice how a room feels, not just how it looks.
Slide 3: Gathering Supplies
Content

Gather:

Microfiber cloths
Approved dusting products
Glass cleaner
Vacuum
Trash bags
Floor cleaning supplies

Bring everything before beginning.

Key Takeaway
Preparation saves time and improves quality.
Slide 4: Preparing The Room
Content

Before cleaning:

Review customer instructions
Remove trash
Identify fragile items
Check for valuables
Note any existing damage

Avoid moving personal items unnecessarily.

Key Takeaway
Respect customer belongings at all times.
Slide 5: Top-To-Bottom Dusting
Content

Always dust from top to bottom.

Example:

Ceiling Fans
↓
Shelves
↓
Pictures
↓
Furniture
↓
Baseboards
↓
Floors

This prevents recontamination.

Key Takeaway
Top-to-bottom cleaning reduces rework.
Slide 6: Furniture Cleaning
Content

Clean:

Tables
End tables
Shelving
Dressers
Nightstands

Remove:

Dust
Fingerprints
Smudges

Use approved products only.

Key Takeaway
Different furniture surfaces may require different care.
Slide 7: Electronics & Screens
Content

Use approved methods only.

Clean carefully:

Television screens
Computer monitors
Remote controls

Never spray chemicals directly onto electronics.

Key Takeaway
Electronics require extra caution.
Slide 8: Decorative Items
Content

Dust carefully:

Picture frames
Decorations
Lamps
Artificial plants

Handle fragile items carefully.

If moved, return them to their original location.

Key Takeaway
Treat decorative items with care and respect.
Slide 9: Blinds & Window Areas
Content

Clean:

Blinds
Window sills
Window tracks (if included)
Accessible trim

Dust often accumulates in these areas.

Key Takeaway
Window areas are commonly overlooked.
Slide 10: Bed Making Standards
Content

If bed making is included:

Straighten linens
Smooth wrinkles
Arrange pillows neatly
Follow company standards

A properly made bed improves room appearance dramatically.

Key Takeaway
A well-made bed creates a professional finish.
Slide 11: Closets & Visible Storage Areas
Content

If included:

Vacuum accessible floor areas
Remove visible debris
Dust accessible shelving

Do not disturb personal belongings.

Key Takeaway
Clean around belongings without reorganizing customer property.
Slide 12: Trash Removal
Content

Remove:

Bedroom trash
Living room trash

Replace liners when required.

Inspect containers before completion.

Key Takeaway
Trash removal is part of a complete service.
Slide 13: Floor Cleaning
Content

After all dusting is complete:

Vacuum carpets
Sweep hard floors
Mop if required

Floor cleaning should be performed last.

Key Takeaway
Clean floors after all other work is complete.
Slide 14: Commonly Missed Areas
Content

Frequently overlooked:

Ceiling fan blades
Baseboards
Under furniture
Lamp shades
Window sills
Light switches
Door handles
Key Takeaway
Details drive customer satisfaction.
Slide 15: Final Inspection & Summary
Content

Before leaving:

Inspect dust-sensitive surfaces
Check floor appearance
Verify trash removal
Confirm room presentation
Ensure bed standards are met

Perform a final walkthrough.

Key Takeaway
Every room should feel refreshed and complete.

Question 1

Why are living rooms and bedrooms important?

A. They contain fewer surfaces

B. Customers spend significant time in them

C. They require no cleaning

D. They are cleaned last

Correct Answer: B

Question 2

What should be done before cleaning begins?

A. Move all furniture

B. Review customer instructions

C. Start vacuuming

D. Dust ceiling fans

Correct Answer: B

Question 3

What cleaning method should be used when dusting?

A. Bottom to top

B. Random order

C. Top to bottom

D. Outside to inside

Correct Answer: C

Question 4

Which item should be dusted first?

A. Baseboards

B. Shelves

C. Ceiling fan

D. Floor

Correct Answer: C

Question 5

When cleaning electronics, employees should:

A. Spray cleaner directly onto screens

B. Use approved cleaning methods

C. Use bathroom disinfectant

D. Use excessive moisture

Correct Answer: B

Question 6

What should happen if a decorative item is moved?

A. Leave it elsewhere

B. Return it to its original location

C. Store it away

D. Throw it away

Correct Answer: B

Question 7

Which area commonly collects dust?

A. Window sills

B. Blinds

C. Ceiling fans

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 8

Why is bed making important?

A. It improves room presentation

B. It saves supplies

C. It reduces dust

D. It changes room size

Correct Answer: A

Question 9

When cleaning closets, employees should:

A. Reorganize belongings

B. Remove customer items

C. Clean accessible areas only

D. Discard unused items

Correct Answer: C

Question 10

What should happen with room trash?

A. Leave it

B. Remove it

C. Hide it

D. Ignore it

Correct Answer: B

Question 11

When should floors be cleaned?

A. First

B. Before dusting

C. Last

D. Before trash removal

Correct Answer: C

Question 12

Which is a commonly missed area?

A. Baseboards

B. Lamp shades

C. Door handles

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 13

What should be verified during final inspection?

A. Room presentation

B. Dust removal

C. Floor appearance

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D

Question 14

How should customer belongings be treated?

A. Reorganized

B. Moved when possible

C. With care and respect

D. Discarded if unnecessary

Correct Answer: C

Question 15

What creates a professional room appearance?

A. Consistent cleaning procedures

B. Proper bed making

C. Attention to detail

D. All of the above

Correct Answer: D
